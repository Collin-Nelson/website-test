A Bit About Me

Background

I am a university student, undergraduate researcher, and engineer at heart. I enjoy all kinds of engineering project work, but I primarily design and build things I need in my daily life. My motivations for projects are to better understand a system or technology and to learn more about each subject, as well as to challenge myself to build each cheaper or better than what is commercially availible. I enjoy mechanical engineering projects, as well as projects that heavily involve other disciplines, including electrical engineering and computer science. I have built everything from a mini-fridge to a 3D printer, and I continue to use and refine these projects as I observe oppertunities for improvement.

Favorite Projects

While I have been a part of many projects, I found some more challenging and engaging than others. I struggle to pick a favorite project, as there are aspects of each that I have really enjoyed. My 3D printer is a top pick because it was my first independent engineering project, and I am thrilled at how well it turned out. On the other hand, I really enjoyed the teamwork that characterized my time in FIRST Robotics, and later in Ri3D as a part of the FIRST Alumni Associaction at NC State. I also had a lot of fun trying to use primerily recycled materials when building my minifridge to be an efficient system, but cost less than half what I could buy a similar unit for.

Hobbies

Outside of my formal responsibilities and project work, I like cycling, camping, hiking, and reading. I regularly cycle to work and classes, as this is significantlty healthier and helps me to be happier and more fufilled. There is a sense of your surroundings that cannot be gained from behind a windshield, and I much prefer to hear, feel, and smell my surroundings, as well as see them. I'd take biking in the rain over traffic any day. I also like to camp by bike, called bicycle touring. This allows me to get a similar experience to backpacking, but see much more, as I can cover over 80 miles a day by bike, vs. only 15 miles a day when hiking. Another intrest of mine is reading. I read everything that sounds interesting to me, from non-fiction tutorial and self-improvement books to fantasy and science fiction. My favorite novel of all time is Ender's Game, which I was initially introduced to when my mother's employer used it as a study on leadership qualities and effective team-building.

I have a plethora of interests, but engineering is my greatest passion. I spend most of my time attending classes to earn my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering, working as an Undergraduate Research Assistant at the Center for Additive Manufacturing and Logistics on NCSU's Centennial Campus, and the other club and personal projects I am involved in.
